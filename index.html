Who We Are

The Pan American Round Table (PART) is a nonprofit woman's organization with member Tables at the local, state, and international levels. PARTT is the Texas state-level organization, with members in twenty Texas cities. As stated in our constitution, the object of this organization is to foster mutual understanding, knowledge, and friendship among the peoples of the Western Hemisphere. PART of Texas and local PART Tables award annual scholarships as a principal means of accomplishing these objectives..

Spring 2026 Issue Vol. 45, No. 4

My goodness, where did the biennium go. I am sure that you will agree that it was as if it was a few days ago that we began this adventure but here it is the end of the biennium. In all truthfulness it was an adventure that was life changing and which provided so many wonderful and enjoyable experiences. In retrospect I would not change any aspect of the biennium, all the table visits, celebrations, new friendships, and the many opportunities to renew old acquaintances. It was my pleasure to be involved in all the events and to interact with so many PART sisters.

The goal that I set for the biennium was to do everything we could to focus our attention and energies on the mission that had been established by our foundress. Mrs. Griswold’s vision, when she established the Pan American Round Table, was to have an organization which would lend assistance to the women and children fleeing the violence in Mexico. The ideals of our organization which are to strive for knowledge, understanding, friendship among the women of the Americas were based on those early goals, aimed at helping women and children. The theme which I choose for the biennium was “Women and Children, a Gift to the World” and I encouraged the Texas Tables to focus their community projects on those benefiting women and children.

It was my delight to learn that Tables across Texas were donating to children’s homes, to women shelters, supplies for schools, canned food for food pantries, coats for children during the winter, diapers for infants, clothing drives and etc. I hope that this trend continues so that in our own small way we can imitate the initial purposes of our early founders.

I am also proud of the accomplishments that were state projects and that we worked together to make successful.

The visit to Florence Terry Griswold birthplace was extremely well attended and could not have happened had not so many assisted in bringing the event about.

The appointment of the former State Directors to an Ad Hoc committee to document unwritten practices by which we operate and which were in need of documenting.

We had an opportunity, along with the McAllen Table, to attend the memorial service for our beloved former State Director Frances V. Metcalf.

The updating and revision of the state constitution that while we have continuously given it attention, there were items that were outdated and needed addressing.

Having been given the honor to serve as the official translator for the Alliance of the Pan American Round Tables which has given me an opportunity to renew old friendships and form new ones.

And lastly, assisting the San Antonio Table to plan for the PARTT 62nd State Convention.

All activities which were brought about through knowledge, understanding and friendship. As you know the popular saying “ If you have done something big you didn’t do it alone” certainly applies to the PARTT state activities. I am so thankful for all the PART members who did not hesitate to assist in accomplishing the goals that make our organization productive. I would like to believe that we all played our parts because we do it “One for All and All for One”. Until we all work together again, thank you for the great memories.

Fondly,

Rosa Maria
